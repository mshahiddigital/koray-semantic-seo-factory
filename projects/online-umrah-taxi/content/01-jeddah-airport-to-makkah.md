## Pickup Instructions at King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED)

King Abdulaziz International Airport operates two main terminals for international arrivals:

**Terminal 1 (International):** Most Umrah and international pilgrims arrive here. After collecting baggage and clearing immigration, exit through the arrivals doors. Your driver stands in the main arrivals hall holding a sign with your name, positioned between columns 3 and 5 near the taxi waiting zone.

**Terminal 2 (Domestic + Some International):** If arriving on a domestic connecting flight, your driver waits at the ground-floor arrivals exit, opposite the currency exchange counters.

**WhatsApp contact on landing:** Before exiting customs, send a WhatsApp message to your driver's Saudi number (provided in your booking confirmation). The driver replies with their exact location in the hall within 5 minutes.

If you cannot locate your driver after 10 minutes, call the 24/7 support number provided in your booking confirmation.

## Miqat Stop for Pilgrims in Ihram

Pilgrims entering Ihram from the direction of Jeddah Airport use **Qarn al-Manazil** (As-Sayl al-Kabir) as the Miqat boundary, located approximately 75 km east of Makkah. This is not on the direct airport-to-Makkah route.

An alternative Miqat available to pilgrims arriving by air is the **Masjid al-Miqat in Jeddah (Masjid al-Hudaybiyah area)**, which some scholars permit for those whose flight path crosses the Miqat boundary at altitude.

If you need to stop at a Miqat location before entering Makkah, notify us at booking. The driver arranges the route accordingly. This adds approximately 30–45 minutes to the journey.

## What Happens If Your Flight Is Delayed

1. We monitor your flight number from booking to landing using live flight tracking.
2. If your flight is delayed by up to 90 minutes — no action needed, no extra charge.
3. If your flight is delayed by more than 90 minutes — your driver contacts you via WhatsApp to confirm the revised arrival time. The driver waits at the confirmed revised time with no penalty.
4. If your flight is cancelled — contact us as soon as you know. Cancellations before the driver departs for the airport incur no charge.

The 90-minute free waiting window starts from your **actual landing time**, not your scheduled arrival time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a taxi from Jeddah Airport to Makkah cost?**
A sedan taxi from King Abdulaziz International Airport (JED) to Makkah costs SAR 240–350 in standard season. An SUV or 7-seat MPV costs SAR 350–480. Prices rise by 20–35% during Ramadan and Hajj peak periods, per market rates observed in February 2026.

**How long does the journey take?**
The journey is approximately 90 km via King Fahd Road and takes 1 hour to 1.5 hours off-peak. Expect 2–3 hours during Ramadan nights, Hajj season traffic, or Friday evening travel.

**Which terminal does my driver meet me at?**
Your driver meets you at the arrivals hall of the terminal you specify at booking — Terminal 1 (most international arrivals) or Terminal 2 (domestic). Provide your terminal in the WhatsApp booking and your driver sends their exact position in the hall.

**Is the price fixed or metered?**
The price is fixed and agreed before the journey. No meter, no hidden tolls, no parking charges added at the end.

**Can I travel with a baby seat or wheelchair?**
Yes. Baby seats and child booster seats are available on request at no additional charge. Wheelchairs fold and fit in the boot of the Hiace van. A driver assistant for pilgrims who need physical help boarding is available — contact us at booking.

**Do you serve female solo travelers?**
Yes. Female pilgrims travel safely in our vehicles. Drivers are uniformed professionals. A female companion in the vehicle can be arranged on request — WhatsApp us to discuss.
