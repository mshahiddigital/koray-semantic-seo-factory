# Makkah to Madinah Taxi — Private Intercity Transfer from SAR 500

**A private taxi from Makkah to Madinah costs SAR 500–650 for a sedan, SAR 700–900 for an SUV or 7-seat MPV, and SAR 900–1,200 for a 9-seat van — covering the full 430 km route with no stops unless requested, fixed pricing, and one complimentary prayer stop.**

The journey takes 4.5 to 5 hours off-peak via King Abdul Aziz Road (Route 60). Travel time extends to 6–7 hours during Ramadan, Hajj season, and when exiting Makkah during congested Haram-area periods, per Saudi Ministry of Roads route data, 2024.

## Travel Time: Makkah to Madinah

The route from Makkah to Madinah is 430 km via Route 60, the King Abdul Aziz Road — the primary intercity highway between the two holy cities.

| Travel condition | Estimated time |
| ---------------- | -------------- |
| Off-peak (weekday, midday) | 4.5 – 5 hours |
| Standard traffic | 5 – 6 hours |
| Ramadan nights / Friday departure | 6 – 7 hours (Makkah exit congestion adds 1–2 hours) |
| Hajj season departure (DhuʼI-Hijjah) | 7+ hours — route management checkpoints apply |

**Best departure times:** Leaving Makkah between 06:00–08:00 or 13:00–15:00 avoids peak Haram congestion. Avoid departing between 08:00–12:00 and 21:00–01:00 during Ramadan.

## Prayer Stops on the Makkah to Madinah Route

Every Makkah to Madinah transfer includes one complimentary prayer stop at a masjid along Route 60. The driver will stop for any fard prayer on request.

Frequently used stops on the route:

- **Masjid al-Safra (Al-Safra township)** — approximately 200 km from Makkah, with a prayer hall, ablution facilities, and parking
- **Al-Hanakiyah Service Area** — approximately 250 km from Makkah, with a masjid, restrooms, and a small café

Specify your prayer stop preference when booking or ask the driver at departure. The stop adds 15–20 minutes to the journey.

## Hotel Pickup in Makkah

The driver collects you from your hotel lobby entrance. For hotels within the restricted Haram perimeter (Abraj Al-Bait towers, Dar Al Tawhid, Swissôtel Makkah, and similar addresses within 500 m of the Masjid al-Haram):

- Pickup is arranged from the nearest vehicle access point outside the pedestrian Haram zone
- The driver contacts you by WhatsApp 30 minutes before pickup to confirm the exact meeting point
- Allow 10–15 minutes to walk from your room to the vehicle access point

For hotels in Aziziyah, Misfalah, Ajyad, and other districts with direct road access — the driver collects from the lobby entrance.

---

## Drop-Off in Madinah

The driver delivers you to your named hotel in Madinah. Hotels near Masjid an-Nabawi (within the Haram boundary pedestrian zone) follow the same arrangement as Makkah — the driver drops at the nearest vehicle access point and contacts you to confirm.

For the Madinah Airport (MED) as a drop-off — the driver goes to the departures drop-off zone, Terminal 1.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a taxi from Makkah to Madinah cost?**
A sedan costs SAR 500–650 in standard season. An SUV or 7-seat MPV costs SAR 700–900. A 9-seat Hiace van costs SAR 900–1,200. Prices increase by 20–30% during Ramadan and Hajj season, per market rates observed in February 2026.

**How long is the drive from Makkah to Madinah?**
The journey is 430 km via Route 60 and takes 4.5 to 5 hours off-peak. Expect 6–7 hours when departing Makkah during Ramadan nights or Hajj season.

**Is the Makkah to Madinah taxi a shared or private service?**
The service is fully private — the vehicle travels exclusively for your group. No shared seats, no additional stops for other passengers.

**Can the driver stop at the Prophet's Mosque (Masjid an-Nabawi) on arrival?**
The driver can drop you at the nearest vehicle access point to your Madinah hotel. Direct access to the pedestrian zone around Masjid an-Nabawi is restricted to vehicles.

**Do I pay the full price for an empty seat?**
No. The price is per vehicle, not per seat. A family of 3 in a sedan pays the sedan price; a couple pays the same sedan price. If you have 7 passengers, book the Hiace at the van rate — not 7 individual seats.

**Is it possible to stop at Bir Ali (Dhul Hulayfah Miqat) on the way to Madinah?**
Yes. The Masjid al-Shajarah at Abyar Ali (Dhul Hulayfah), approximately 9 km southwest of Madinah, is the Miqat for pilgrims exiting Madinah toward Makkah. If you need this stop, specify it at booking. The stop adds 20–30 minutes to the journey.
