# HCU + Quality Audit — Online Umrah Taxi

## 1) Helpfulness (task completion)
- Does the page clearly answer: *Can I book? How? What happens next?*
- Does it cover constraints: pickup points, waiting, luggage, group sizes, prayer stops, traffic?
- Does it prevent mistakes: meeting point, terminal, communication steps?

## 2) Experience signals (E-E-A-T)
- Clear business identity (name, support hours, contact)
- Policy clarity (cancellation, flight delay, refunds)
- Service boundaries (what is/isn’t included)
- Evidence of real operations (photos, fleet details, process)

## 3) Content integrity
- No unverified numeric claims (prices/times/distances) without a maintained source
- No overpromises (e.g., “fastest always”, “guaranteed time”)—use realistic qualifiers
- No duplicate pages for minor keyword variants

## 4) Local & religious sensitivity
- Respectful wording (Hajj/Umrah context)
- Clarity on stop needs (Miqat, prayer, rest)
- Special assistance options (elderly/wheelchair) stated honestly

## 5) Conversion UX
- Above-the-fold CTA
- WhatsApp + form + phone (if used)
- Clear fields: route, date, time, flight number, passengers, luggage, vehicle preference

## 6) Structured data (where applicable)
- `LocalBusiness` or `TaxiService` / `TransportationService`
- `Service`
- `FAQPage` (for FAQs)
- `BreadcrumbList`

## 7) Internal linking
- Hub/spoke compliance per `network/internal-linking-plan.md`
- No orphan pages

## 8) Content depth (semantic coverage)
- Entities covered: cities, airports, routes, vehicle types, passenger/luggage capacity, policies
- FAQs match real user questions (pricing, pickup, delays, luggage)
