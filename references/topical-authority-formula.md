## Core Formula
Topical Authority = **Coverage × Historical Data × Cost of Retrieval** + **Momentum + Depth + Vastness + Updateness**

## Breakdown & Scoring (0–100 per Component)
1. **Coverage (30% weight)** – Semantic completeness of EAV triples, query networks, lexical relations. Score: % of competitor EAV covered (e.g., 80% = 24 points).
2. **Historical Data (20%)** – Site/page age, update history, backlinks over time. Score: Number of historical signals (e.g., "since 2023" mentions, GSC trends).
3. **Cost of Retrieval (20%)** – Low-latency signals: semantic HTML, structured data, fast crawl budget. Score: Proxies like page speed + entity resolution ease.
4. **Momentum (10%)** – Traffic/user engagement growth. Score: GSC click curves + behavioral data.
5. **Depth (10%)** – Detailed EAV attributes per entity. Score: Average paragraph depth (e.g., 5+ qualifiers/examples).
6. **Vastness (5%)** – Breadth of related entities/topics. Score: Number of outer sections/bridge topics.
7. **Updateness (5%)** – Freshness signals. Score: "As of 2026" + update frequency.

## How to Calculate Total Score
- Sum weighted components (max 100)
- Threshold for "unbreakable": 85+ (outperforms Wikipedia in niche)

## Best Practices
- Always prioritize Core Section for Coverage.
- Use Python scripts for automated scoring (e.g., advertools for EAV coverage).
- In audits: If score <70, flag for rework.

Examples:
- Niche: Electric Scooters – Coverage 90% (high EAV), Historical 50% (new site) → Total ~75 (needs historical nodes).
