## Core Philosophy (Always Remember)
- One macro context per page (central entity + source context)
- Immediate factual answers + EAV (Entity-Attribute-Value) everywhere
- Algorithmic Authorship signals: proper word sequence, factual tone, no fluff, user-first helpfulness
- Topical Authority formula support: Coverage × Historical Data × Cost of Retrieval + Depth/Vastness/Momentum/Updateness

## 14 CORE RULES (Apply to EVERY sentence/paragraph)
1. **Proper Word Sequence** – Put the most important information first. Main subject + verb + key attribute immediately.
2. **Be Certain & Factual** – Use only proven facts. Never use “should”, “will”, “probably”, “might”. State as absolute truth.
3. **Cut All Fluff & Metadiscourse** – Remove “In this article”, “As we can see”, “It is important to note”, “You will learn”.
4. **Use Numeric Values & Qualifiers** – Always add numbers, dates, percentages, quantities, durations when possible.
5. **Qualify Instances** – After any plural noun, immediately give concrete examples (e.g. “Popular models include X, Y, Z”).
6. **Correct Verb Context** – Use precise verbs (improve → enhance performance; develop → create from scratch).
7. **Examples After Plural Nouns** – Every plural list must be followed by real-world examples in the same paragraph.
8. **Same Part-of-Speech (POS) Consistency in Lists** – All items in a bullet/list must be same grammatical form (all nouns, all verbs, etc.).
9. **Immediate Answer Under Every Heading** – First 30–45 words under any H2/H3 must directly answer the heading/question.
10. **Bold the Answer, Not the Search Term** – Bold the factual answer/value, never just the keyword.
11. **One Macro Context Per Page** – Entire page must stay within single central entity and source context (no jumping topics).
12. **“If” Statements in Second Part** – Conditional sentences: condition first, result second (If X happens, then Y occurs).
13. **Anchor Text Matches Target Title Exactly** – Internal links use the exact target page title as anchor text.
14. **Relevant Entities & Semantically Related Words** – Naturally weave EAV triples and lexical relations (hypernyms, hyponyms, verbs-of-life).

## 28 ADVANCED PATTERNS (Micro-Semantics & Ranking Edges)
15. **Paragraph Function Optimization** – First sentence = main claim, middle = evidence/EAV, last = implication or next step.
16. **Subordinate Text First-Sentence Rule** – Every sub-section’s opening sentence must be self-contained answer.
17. **No Negative Phrases** – Never write “don’t”, “avoid”, “never”, “bad”. Rephrase positively.
18. **Positive & Empathetic Tone** – Always helpful, encouraging, user-focused language.
19. **EAV Triple Integration** – Every paragraph must contain at least one explicit or implicit Entity-Attribute-Value.
20. **Verbs-of-Life Flow** – Use action verbs that connect user journey (discover → learn → apply → achieve → earn).
21. **Source Context Reinforcement** – Mention origin/authority (researchers, studies, patents, real cases).
22. **Information Gain Focus** – Add unique data, comparisons, or angles not found on competing pages.
23. **Heading Vector Alignment** – Heading must be exact user query or very close semantic match.
24. **Safe Answer Principle** – Never over-promise; use “according to”, “studies show”, “in 2025 data”.
25. **Numeric Density** – Target 1–2 numbers/qualifiers per 100 words.
26. **List Optimization** – Maximum 7 items per list; always introduce with purpose sentence.
27. **Internal Linking Rule** – Link only to pages within the same topical map; use exact title.
28. **Title & Meta Alignment** – Page title must contain central entity + primary attribute.
29. **URL Structure** – /central-entity/primary-attribute/ (kebab-case).
30. **Schema & Structured Data Readiness** – Content must naturally support FAQ, HowTo, Article, or EAV Schema.
31. **Readability for Humans + Machines** – Average sentence 18–22 words; short paragraphs (3–5 lines).
32. **No Contextless Words** – Every adjective/adverb must add measurable value.
33. **Historical Data Signals** – Mention dates, previous versions, “since 2023”, “updated in 2026”.
34. **User Behavior Alignment** – Write for dwell time: answer fast, then deepen.
35. **Momentum Building** – End sections with clear next-step or related topic teaser.
36. **Depth Layering** – Start broad → add specifics → end with advanced tips.
37. **Vastness Support** – Cover multiple sub-attributes in outer sections.
38. **Updateness** – Include “as of February 2026” or current-year references.
39. **Evidence-Based** – Cite real sources, studies, patents, or case studies.
40. **Algorithmic Authorship Signals** – Consistent author voice, factual precision, entity prominence.
41. **Final Polish** – Read aloud: must sound like an expert human who deeply understands the topic.
42. **HCU Compliance** – Content must demonstrably help the user achieve their goal better than any other page.
